## 2. Provenance

- Why this category is needed:
  It supports the paper's argument that provenance is necessary for object linkage and derivation context, but not sufficient on its own for the paper's minimal accountability object.
- How many core citations are enough:
  2 to 4 core citations are enough.
- What claim in the paper it is meant to support:
  The method and related-work claim that provenance is one required component within the profile, but provenance-only approaches do not automatically bind policy, minimal evidence, and validation into one locally checkable statement.

- Why this category is needed:

  2 to 3 core citations are enough.
- What claim in the paper it is meant to support:
  The comparison claim that policy contributes rule basis but does not by itself establish which operation occurred, whether references close, or how validation should be performed for a concrete statement.

## 4. Profile / Conformance / Validation

- Why this category is needed:
  It supports the strongest software-engineering part of the paper: the move from a profile definition to profile-aware checking, explicit failure categories, and reproducible artifact packaging.
- How many core citations are enough:
  3 to 5 core citations are enough.
- What claim in the paper it is meant to support:
  The central methodology claim that a narrow profile and a profile-aware validation path form a more defensible engineering contribution than schema-only description or unconstrained JSON exchange.
