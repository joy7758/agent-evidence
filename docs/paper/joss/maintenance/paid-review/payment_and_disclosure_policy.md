# Payment and Disclosure Policy

Date: 2026-06-01

## Payment Scope

Payment compensates a reviewer or contributor for time spent on a defined task.
It does not buy a positive conclusion, a favorable review, GitHub popularity,
authorship, citation, or publication outcome.

Each paid task should have:

- a written task statement;
- a public GitHub issue or PR deliverable;
- clear acceptance criteria;
- a small fixed budget or capped hours;
- no requirement for praise or positive findings.

## Public Work

Paid work for JOSS readiness should be public by default:

- reproduction notes in GitHub issues;
- documentation or test changes in PRs;
- review comments in PR threads;
- command results included in the public record.

Do not use private evidence files, private media, or confidential logs.

## Disclosure

Where relevant, describe paid work as paid external review or paid
contribution. Reviewers should disclose conflicts of interest if they exist.

Suggested wording:

> This issue/PR was produced as a paid external review or paid contribution.
> Payment compensated time spent on reproduction, review, or contribution work;
> it did not require positive conclusions.

## Integrity Rules

Do not pay for:

- stars or popularity metrics;
- fake issues;
- fake PRs;
- generic praise;
- promised acceptance;
- claimed adoption without evidence.

Do not ask contributors to broaden AEP-Media's claim boundary. Negative
findings, failed reproduction, and criticism are valid and useful outcomes.
